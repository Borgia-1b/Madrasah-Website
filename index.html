History

Madrasah school in Davao city is often used to describe Muslim private institutions that place
a strong focus on Arabic and Islamic education. It is a privately run institution that depends
on donations from the local populace or from abroad, mainly from Islamic or Muslim nations and
the history of is Muslim private schools that place a strong focus on Islamic studies and Arabic
literacy are known as Madrasah. It depends on donations from the local populace or from abroad,
mainly from Islamic or Muslim nations or the government.


Organizer

The organization responsible for founding this school is the local government of Davao City
and is currently being managed by the Department of Education (Dep-Ed). The reason for this
is so the children in the area are able to have access to free or less expensive education.
Education is a priority around the world however the Philippines is still a developing country
and not everyone can have access to schools. So by having schools where the government pays
for all the essentials all children can have access to education.


Beneficiaries

The beneficiaries of the organizations are Muslims as Madrasah is a community-based
institution that teaches quality education in the Arabic language and Islam which is
culturally-sensitive, Islamic friendly, and give them equal work opportunity.


Benefactors

The organization that provides support to the institution is the Department of Education.
This helps the institution become more efficient by having more teachers and donators to
help make the school more productive and make learning easier for the children. The
teachers and students can also have more materials needed for their lessons like books,
notebooks, pencils, ballpen, etc. so that they can teach more local and foreign students
that live here in the Philippines. The students benefit from this because they can enhance
their knowledge of their own culture despite being in another country and at the same
time they can also learn about the culture of the Philippines.
